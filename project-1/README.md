## Project Overview

This project involved creating an interactive Power BI dashboard to assess sales performance of 45 Walmart stores in the US between February 2010 and October 2012. The analysis focused on year-over-year sales trends during the recovery from the 2008 recession, incorporating calendar and conditional data modeling to enhance insights.

---

## Business Problem

Walmart stores, like many discount retailers, faced fluctuating sales during the post-2008 recession period. Understanding sales trends and external factors such as holidays and unemployment rates was critical to optimizing store performance and strategic planning.

---

## Objective

- Analyze year-over-year sales trends across multiple Walmart stores.
- Incorporate external factors like holidays and unemployment rates into sales analysis.
- Develop a dynamic Power BI dashboard for clear visualization and decision-making.

---

## Tools & Technologies

- Power BI Desktop
- DAX (Data Analysis Expressions)
- Excel
- Power Query Editor
- CSV Data Files

---

## Project Workflow

- Import Walmart sales CSV data into Power BI Desktop.
- Create calendar and refresh date tables using DAX.
- Establish relationships between sales and calendar tables.
- Create conditional columns for holiday flags and unemployment rates.
- Develop DAX measures for total sales, average sales, and year-over-year growth.

---

## Key Insights

- Sales showed variability influenced by holiday weeks and unemployment rates.
- Year-over-year sales growth highlighted recovery trends post-recession.
- Certain months consistently outperformed others, indicating seasonal patterns.
- Data modeling and custom sorting improved clarity of sales trend analysis.
