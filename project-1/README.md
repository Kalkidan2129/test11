## Project Overview

This project involved creating a detailed sales dashboard for 45 Walmart stores in the US, covering the period from February 2010 to October 2012. Using Power BI, the analysis focused on year-over-year sales performance during the recovery phase following the 2008 recession. The dashboard integrates sales data with calendar and economic indicators to provide actionable insights.

---

## Business Problem

Walmart stores, like many discount retailers, experienced fluctuating sales during the post-2008 recession recovery period. Understanding these sales trends was critical to identifying factors affecting store performance and optimizing inventory and marketing strategies. The challenge was to analyze and visualize sales data effectively to support data-driven decision-making.

---

## Objective

- Analyze year-over-year sales trends across multiple Walmart stores.
- Integrate economic indicators such as unemployment rates and holiday periods into sales analysis.
- Develop an interactive Power BI dashboard to visualize sales performance and support business insights.

---

## Tools & Technologies

- Power BI Desktop
- DAX (Data Analysis Expressions)
- Excel
- Power Query Editor
- CSV Data Files

---

## Project Workflow

- Import Walmart sales CSV data into Power BI Desktop.
- Create and refresh calendar and date tables for time intelligence.
- Establish relationships between sales data and calendar tables.
- Develop conditional columns for holiday flags and unemployment rates.
- Build DAX measures to calculate total sales, average sales, and year-over-year growth.

---

## Key Insights

- Sales across Walmart stores showed varied recovery patterns post-recession from 2010 to 2012.
- Holiday weeks had a measurable impact on weekly sales performance.
- Unemployment rates influenced sales trends, reflecting broader economic conditions.
- Custom sorting and calendar integration improved the accuracy of time-based sales analysis.
